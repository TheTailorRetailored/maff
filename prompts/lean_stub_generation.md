Claim-centric Maff rule:
- A theorem, conjecture, lemma, proposition, corollary, reduction, or counterexample statement is a Claim.
- Claims form the recursive mathematical proof graph.
- Routes are usually sections inside Claims.
- Tasks are operational queue records attached to Claims, not graph nodes.
- Proof attempts and minor gaps are usually inline unless substantial.
- Create graph nodes only for mathematical objects or substantial independent artifacts.
- Do not create star graphs with every object attached directly to the Problem.
Output: imports, Lean theorem statement, local definitions, lemma stubs, and assumptions explicitly marked.
